Visiting Researcher | Harvard & MITFelix Sosa

I am a NSF LSAMP scholar at the University of Central Florida studying computer science and mathematics. I want to advance the fields of cognitive science and artificial intelligence through modelling human cognition and evolution. I am currently a visiting research at MIT and Harvard under the advisement of Dr. Sam Gershman, Dr. Tobias Gerstenberg, and Dr. Tomer Ullman working on social cognition and transfer learning. At UCF I'm under the advisement of Dr. Kenneth Stanley where I work on developing open-ended neuroevolution algorithms.

In my spare time I make an effort to start and lead educational efforts for undergraduates and graduates on how to get involved in research and general artificial intelligence.

Research & Projects

I currently do research in two domains with the hopes of developing bridges between the two in the future to help solve intelligence. Within evolutionary computation, I work with open-ended neuroevolution developing algorithms that can generate artificial neural network topologies given a problem domain. Within computational cognitive science, I work with social cognition developing models of morality inference and transfer learning.

Throughout my undergraduate career, I've been involved in multiple projects applying machine learning to solve interesting problems in domains like bioinformatics and brain-computer interfacing.

NSF CBMM: MIT Summer Research Program

I was accepted into the 2017 MIT Summer Research Program. I work under the advisement of Dr. Sam Gershman, Dr. Tobias Gerstenberg, and Dr. Tomer Ullman working on social cognition and transfer learning.

NSF CBMM QBW: Teaching Assistant

I was a Teaching Assistant for the Center for Brains, Minds, and Machine's 2017 Quantitative Biology Workshop. I lectured and taught students the basics of Python and computational thinking. I aided other TAs lecturing on concepts of machine learning, image processing, and other quantitative methods.

UCF Student Undergraduate Research Council: Council Member

I was accepted to join UCF's SURC to work with administration to improve and broaden on campus efforts to get undergraduates involved in research at UCF.
