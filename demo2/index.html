Research & Projects

I currently do research in two domains with the hopes of bridging the two into a theoretical framework for intelligence: evolutionary computation and computational cognitive science. Within evolutionary computation, I design open-ended neuroevolutionary systems - algorithms that can unboundedly generate artificial neural networks given a domain. Within computational cognitive science, I currently work with social cognition developing models of moral judgment.

Additionally, I've been involved in multiple projects applying machine learning to solve interesting problems in domains like bioinformatics and brain-computer interfacing.

SIG Artificial Intelligence

I founded and currently lead a series of lectures and workshops on artificial intelligence that aim to teach intuitions on the nuances of intelligence research that are usually left outisde of the classroom and promote a vibrant community of AI researchers within the UCF student body. I run this series alongside some of the best and brightest friends I've ever had.

Together we've been able to secure funding and sponsorship for the lecture series for the Fall 2016 and Spring 2017 semesters from the NSF Center for Brains, Minds, and Machines - hosted at MIT - and Intel through their Artificial Intelligence Academy.

Check out our GitHub: SIGAI HackPack

NSF CBMM Quantitative Methods Workshop at MIT

I've been invited to lecture at the 2017 and 2018 NSF Quanititative Methods Workshop - an outreach program by the NSF that brings students for a week to MIT to learn computational principles such as programming and data processing methods. I taught students from multiple institutions how to program in Python and aided other lecturers in teaching concepts from machine learning, audio processing, and image processing.

Teaching here was a life changing experience and, if I could, I'd do it every year.

Check out the site: CBMM QMW

Synthetic Biology

Before getting involved in computer science - when I used to formally study Biology and Chemistry - I founded and led an organization similar to SIGAI, but for Synthetic Biology. We hosted lectures and workshops on genetic engineering concepts and methods that would allow students to get real, attractive, hands-on experience at the bench working with the field's latest methods like CRISPR-Cas9.

The organization was a response to my frustrations not having enough experience to be competitive for lab positions at UCF. It now is succesful and running smoothly in the hands of other students who share a similar vision.

Check out the Facebook group: Syn Bio
