Research

UCF

At UCF I work under Dr. Kenneth Stanley in the Evolutionary Complexity Lab where we focus on neuroevolution. Specifically, my research is based on the problem of open-ended neuroevolution.

How can we have an artificial neural network learn its own optimal topology - or shape - as well as its own optimal weights wihtout giving any prior knowledge to the neural network other than the problem it ought to solve; this is the direct - oversimplified - question my current research attempts, and excitingly seems, to answer.

Outreach

CBMM QBW

I was a Teaching Assistant for the Center for Brains, Minds, and Machine's 2017 Quantitative Biology Workshop. I lectured and taught students the basics of Python and computational thinking and aided other TAs lecturing on concepts of machine learning, image processing, and other quantitative methods.

SIGAI @ UCF

At my home university I run a student organization called SIG AI where I deliver a lecture/workshop series for the semester to undergraduate and graduate students on big picture concepts in artificial intelligence. In the Fall of 2016 the lecture series was sponsored by the Center for Brains, Minds, and Machines and this Spring 2017 it was sponsored by the CBMM and Intel. You can watch our lectures on our YouTube channel.

Synthetic Biology @ UCF

In the Summer of 2015 I founded a student organization dedicated to teaching the concepts of genetic engineering and synthetic biology to UCF's students through lectures and hands-on workshops dealing with the field's most advanced methods and technology. Our lectures have gone over topics like gene therapy and longevity research and we've hosted workshops that have given students hands-on experience genetically modifying model organisms utilizing CRIPSR-Cas9 among other methods.
